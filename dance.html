Abba Modern

"Glory" - Fall 2020

Abba Modern's fall 2020 set, filmed virtually. This was my first semester acting as executive director, videographer, editor, and choreographer for Abba Modern. Due to COVID-19, the team pivoted towards a virtual format and a video project for our end-of-semester deliverable. The process of creating this video was long, but gratifying, from the choreography, to the storyboarding, to the filming, to the editing. This video is an ode to this semester's team, who stuck it out even in the midst of a pandemic and virtual semester of college. This piece is about being able to stand up, go forward, and continue running our races even in times of struggle and hardship, because we know that our God is a good God.

"Victory" - Spring 2020

Abba Modern's spring 2020 set, filmed virtually. This semester was cut short due to the sudden spike of COVID-19. As such, this set, meant to be for stage, was reformatted to video. I had the pleasure of choreographing the second section with LoriAnn Kim, and got to explore isolations and hard hitting movement. As chief editor of this video, there were many sections that were unchoreographed and little to no planned transitions, so I used this opportunity to showcase what could have been, using practice videos from pre-COVID times. This piece is about the struggle of complacency, confusion, and general spiritual warfare, and how in the end, we can proudly declare Jesus wins the victory.

"Extravagant" - Fall 2019

Abba Modern's fall 2019 set, performed at Danceworx and AFX showcases. I had the immense pleasure of choreographing two separate sections for this set, one in collaboration with James Shin, and the other in collaboration with Aileen Gui. This set allowed me the opportunity to explore both open-style and contemporary movement, as well as learn more about the choreography and teaching processes. While it definitely pushed me to my limits, I was able to grow a lot from this set that is meant to be pure worship to God.

Comin' In Hot" - Fall 2019

A concept video welcoming the new Abba Modern pledge class and showcasing our Fall 2019 open-style audition piece. The highest production shoot I have ever been apart of, it was nice to go back to my roots and dance to something open-style. Although I usually do not act like a "cool" or "smug" character, it was very fun to channel that side of myself during this shoot. I'm definitely more comfortable with this style of open-style, so it was a very fun shoot that helped me return to the original style of dance I began with.

"Redeemed" - Spring 2019

Abba Modern's set for the spring 2019 semester and my very first live dance set I ever performed. This set will always have a special place in my heart because it was the set that made me fall in love with open-style dance. I was able to choreograph the closer piece with Sarah Song and Rebekah Tang, and learned so much from every single team member. This set's story follows a man strayed away from God listening to the testimony of his friend and learning that he can be redeemed by the grace of Christ.

"A Million Pieces" - Spring 2019

A video project led by Christy Tak. My first encounter with contemporary dance and aiding in the video making process. Although I have no semblance of technique or formal training, it was extremely fun and challenging to push my body with a different style of altogether. This project portrays the redemptive power of Jesus Christ and humanity's reliance on God. It was such an interesting learning process, trying to adapt to the outdoors, but I am very pleased with the end result.

"Beloved" - Spring 2019

My first video project with Abba Modern, led by Sarah Song. With this video, I felt so powerfully that dance was being used as more than just bodily movement, but as a vehicle that portrayed emotion, story, and visionary art. With the style being a combination of open-style and contemporary dance, it was definitely a push for me to learn, though I am very grateful for the growth this provided. It was an incredible privilege to be apart of this project.

TrueLement

"TL HOME - TrueLement Fall 2020" - Fall 2019

A fun video project I was able to participate in. This team really became a family for me and so even during a completely virtual semester, I decided to return and I actually enjoyed my time with the team so much. Exploring different styles, including hip-hop, isolations, popping, and general open-style movement, this video project is meant to be light hearted and convey what the Fall 2020 season has been like for TrueLement at home during the COVID-19 pandemic.

"TrueLement Presents TL;DR" - Spring 2020

My first set with TrueLement. Since starting open-style dance in college, I aspired to join TrueLement, and during the spring 2020 semester, I was finally accepted. So many of my inspirations and role models in the Berkeley Dance Community are apart of this team, so it was truly a blessing and privilege to be apart of this team. I'm very proud of the fact that, although the circumstances of the COVID-19 coronavirus cut the time this team had short, we were able to put this together in 4 weeks.

The [M]ovement

"[M]JUS2" - Fall 2019

My first live set with The [M]ovement; an open-style video set directed by Jennifer Xie and Beini Liu. Filled with nostaglic songs from the early 2010s, this set reminisiced on the musical hits of our middle school era. Through this team, I was able to make many of my now closest friends and was able to fall in love with dance again. Overall, the upbeat, groovey, and energetic choreography fit my style well. It was refreshing just to dance to pop music and perform a "feel-good" set!

"[M]omentum VP" - Summer 2019

My first experience with The [M]ovement; an open-style video project led by Alyssa Yee. This video portrays the need to break out of the melancholy of routine everyday life. The experience of participating in this video project was incredible , since I could see so clearly how Alyssa was trying to portray her story through the choreography. It was truly an honor to be apart of such a relatable and meaningful piece.

AFX

"AFXsi Reflect" - Summer 2019

My first time dancing with an AFX training team, specifically under Grace Lee, Alicia Chow, and Soo Rim Choi. With this team being a "training" team, I was able to go back to fundamentals and train the basics of dance that I may have neglected. The opportunity to dance on Reflect allowed me to also create lifelong friendships, as I was able to expand my network within the Berkeley Dance Community. This set portrays the different ways one might look at themselves in the mirror, but ends with embracing oneself for who they are. A pure joy to be apart of.
